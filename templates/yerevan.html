Pink City Yerevan, Armenia

About Yerevan

Yerevan, the capital of Armenia, is one of the world’s oldest cities with a history dating back to 782 BC. This vibrant and welcoming city blends ancient history with modern lifestyle. From pink-hued Soviet architecture to cozy outdoor cafes and a thriving arts scene, Yerevan offers a unique experience for visitors seeking culture, history, and hospitality in one destination.

Around Yerevan

Explore the scenic beauty and historical wonders just outside Yerevan. Take a day trip to Garni Temple, Geghard Monastery, Lake Sevan, or the ancient ruins of Zvartnots. Around Yerevan, you’ll find breathtaking landscapes, medieval monasteries, and authentic Armenian villages that enrich your travel experience beyond the city limits.

Top 10 Things to Do in Yerevan

  • Climb the Cascade Complex – Enjoy panoramic views of Yerevan and Mount Ararat from the top of this iconic staircase and art center.
  • Visit Republic Square – Marvel at the architecture and watch the colorful dancing fountains at night.
  • Explore the History Museum of Armenia – Discover thousands of years of Armenian history through fascinating exhibits.
  • Walk along Northern Avenue – A modern pedestrian street lined with shops, cafes, and live performances.
  • Relax at the Opera House and Freedom Square – A cultural hub with beautiful surroundings and great photo opportunities.
  • Shop at Vernissage Flea Market – Buy handmade souvenirs, carpets, jewelry, and antiques.
  • Tour the Armenian Genocide Memorial (Tsitsernakaberd) – Learn about the country’s tragic past and pay respects at this solemn site.
  • Taste authentic Armenian food – Try khorovats (barbecue), dolma, lavash bread, and local wine at traditional restaurants.
  • Stroll through Lovers’ Park – A quiet and romantic green space ideal for a peaceful break.
  • Experience Yerevan’s nightlife – Enjoy local music, live jazz, and rooftop bars in the city center.
